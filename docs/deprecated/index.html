Chaconne by JS Bach. I know, it's an obsession. 1 theme of 8 bars (white) + 32 variations numbered from 1 to 32 (in colours) + a final chord (black).

This count of variations (32 )can be questioned; some will say less (31), others more (33, even 34), the debate is legitimate. At some point one has to stop wracking his brains, quit intellectual (monkey spanking / chicken choking / pipe cleaning), and make a decision. Done!

The theme is in D minor, and so are the first 16 variations, then 10 variations are in D major (Δ), then 6 final variations are back to D minor. Most of the variations are 8 bars long, except for variations 8 and 31 (12 bars), and variations 10, 16, 19 and 30 (4 bars).

Bottom line: Theme: 8 bars first group, *16* variations in D minor, #1 to #16: 132 - 8 = *128* bars second group, *10* variations in D major, #17 to #26: 208 - 132 = *76* bars third group, *6* variations in D minor, #27 to #32: 256 - 208 = *48* bars

Now, the proportions of the 3 groups of variations. 1) In number of bars: first group / second group = 128 / 76 ~= *1.68* second group / third group = 76 / 48 ~= *1.58*

2) In number of variations: first group / second group= 16 / 10 = *1.60* second group / third group = 10 / 6 ~= *1.67* We can see that all these ratios are close to each other: *1.68* ~= *1.58* ~= *1.60* ~= *1.67*

*Golden Ratio!* "Two quantities are in the golden ratio if their ratio is the same as the ratio of their sum to the larger of the two quantities". Which may be written: ( a + b ) / a = a / b with a > b. https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Golden_ratio

Three lines of elementary algebra later, we can express the ratio a/b, i.e. the golden ratio, denoted φ, as the unique positive solution of the equation φ² = φ + 1. That is, φ ~= *1.618*
